Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was also the president of India from 2002 to 2007. The vision he had for India and his work towards it will be remembered by many and it will continue to inspire generations to come.

1931 Born to a Tamil Muslim family, youngest of five children in his family
1940* Started working as a Newspaper Boy at a young age to supplement his family's income
1951* Completed his schooling with average grades although was seen as a hardworking kid with a strong desire to learn
1954 Completed his Graduation in Physics from St. Joseph's College Tiruchirappalli
1955 Joined Madras Institute of Technology and studied Aerospace Engineering
1958* Narrowly missed his dream of becoming a fighter pilot; of eight positions available in the IAF, he qualified ninth
1960 Joined DRDO as Research Scientist in 1960 and designed small Helicopters, Super Sonic Missiles and Hovercrafts
1969 Appointed as Director of India's first SLV Project in ISRO
1981* Started working on Advance & Guided Missiles, on the suggestion of Indira Gandhi
1998* Secretly conducted Operation Shakti and made a seat of Atomic Power
1999* Declined the offer of Cabinet Minister by Atal Vajpayee. He was Chief Scientific Advisor to the PM
2002 Elected as People's President
2007 Moved out of President House with the same 2 suitecases and few books that he moved in with. Donated all his assets
2010* Joined as Visiting Professor in Universities and committed rest of his life to Youth and Kids
2015 Left for his Heavenly Abode during his lecture at IIM-Shilong and laid to rest at Pei Karumbu

* = Could not find the exact years
